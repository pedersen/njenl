Getting Started

What's the point of this game?

Each side is struggling to capture the minds of humanity to push forward its own goals. To capture the minds, we build fields covering the areas where people live and work and play. The fields are three portals (sites of special interest for some reason), linked together, covering an area. Each side is working to establish their fields, and keep their fields established, so as to help further their side's goal and bring about their ultimate victory.

This, though, is only part of the game. Many people get to the level cap (level 8) and stop, thinking they've done everything there is to do. They are missing so much more, as this game has a major social component too. People from Russia and Australia have worked closely with people from New York City during operations. Friendships have been made. Places have been discovered that were unknown before. People have learned more about their home towns in the year that Ingress has been up than they ever did for the years before it became reality.

It's a game, yes. But it's more than that. It's a way and a reason to get out and meet people. We hope you'll join us.

How do I get started?
First, you have to have the application. You can find it at the Play Store. Once you install it, sign in, and pick your agent name. Choose Enlightened as your faction, and go through the training missions. You're now started.
What do I do next?

Now, you want to look at the the intel map for your area. It will show you the portals that exist around. Get out. Go visit them. Get some gear. Claim some portals. Meet people. And, above all else, have fun.

We recommend doing two other things as well to help you get into the game. First, check out Ingress Boot Camp and the Ingress Field Guide. It has many more tips for getting started, along with strategy guides and information about the back story of the game. Second, check out our New Jersey communities. In there, you will find some locals who will be happy to help you in a number of ways. They really are your best resource.

Getting Gear

How do I get gear?

There are two methods to get gear. The first (and most preferred) is to hack portals. Hack every single one of them. If you are hacking an enemy portal, you will get gear and AP (which helps you level up faster). If you hack your own faction's portal, you get slightly more gear (usually). To hack a portal, click on it on your scanner, and then click "Hack".

The second method is a gear drop. Find someone local to you, and ask them for gear. Frequently, they will be happy to help you out. Even if they can't, they can usually point you to someone who will help you out.

Getting AP (How To Level Up)

How can I gain more AP?

At the lowest levels, taking down enemy portals can be very difficult. Gaining enough AP to go up can seem impossible. The best thing to do if you wish to rise rapidly is to contact someone local to you, and ask them for help. They'll take down enemy portals, leaving them grey for you to capture. Once you've started capturing portals, make links between them. This will give you still more AP. Once you're able to establish links among a group of portals, you'll make fields, which give you still more AP. Later, you'll be able to take down enemy portals with ease, allowing you to gain still more AP entirely on your own.

Note: If you have someone clearing enemy portals for you, listen to their advice about links to make. They'll help you make the most fields possible, which will result in your getting the biggest AP gain possible.

Capturing Portals

How do I capture a neutral (grey) portal?
Once you have found the portal, go stand near to it. There is a ring around your avatar on the screen. Try to get the edge of that ring as close to the center of the portal as possible, and hit deploy. That will space the resonators out as far as possible, making it harder for someone else to take it down.
How do I capture an enemy (blue) portal?
You will need XMPs to take down an enemy portal. XMPs have range that is affected by their level: The higher the level, the farther away they will do real damage. When starting out, the best thing to do is to move around until you are standing on the resonator, and then fire your XMPs. Long press on the screen until the menu comes up, and then click "Fire XMP". Select the XMP, then press and hold the "Fire" button until the white ring collapses in the center. Release it then for an attack bonus of 20%. If you release too soon, you will still get an attack bonus, just not the maximum 20%.

The Social Game

How do I talk to other players?

The game itself provides a mechanism. When running, there is a tab at the bottom of the screen labeled "COMM". Tap that, and a window slides up with three tabs: "All", "Faction", and "Alerts". If you are talking to a faction mate, make sure to use only the Faction tab. It's mildly more secure than All (both sides, sadly, have players with one Enlightened account and one Resistance account, with the alternate account used for spying). If you want to talk to them more securely, then you're going to need to use something like instant messaging or email. Most Ingress players use Google+ and Hangouts for communication, and we recommend it.

When using the in game Comm system, send a message to a specific player by using an @ in front of their name. For instance, "@Shadowfall hello" will send that message to Shadowfall regardless of where he is. Since distances can matter very much, we recommend using an @mention whenever possible.

How do I meet other players?

For most people, that's easy: Simply ask them in comms to meet. Use a public place, like a nearby Dunkin' Donuts, or Wawa, or pub. Do all the usual safe meeting practices you would do for meeting someone for the first time, then go there, say hi, and swap stories. They want to meet you just as much as you want to meet them, especially if they're higher level players. We are all stronger when we are a team, and including lower level players is something the higher level players very much want to do.

Note: If they do not wish to meet, do not try to force it. That is against the terms of service (as it is harassment) and could get you banned.

How do I verify the identity of other players I have never met?
Verifying the identity of other players is fairly easy, but time consuming. We recommend following the steps posted by ShadowFall to do it.
How do I know who I can trust if I never meet them?
Trust is actually a very hard thing to do. Sometimes, you might find yourself needing to trust someone half a world away, and have no way of verifying you should. You can verify that their identity on Google+ is the owner of the agent name on Ingress, but how can you tell that they are a legitimate player, and not a double account? To do that, you have to network yourself, and talk to more and more people from farther and farther away. Introduce yourself, get others to introduce you, and keep plugging away. But that will only help the other side know you. How will you know them? Only via the chain of introductions. There's nothing better than that.
